# memex — Vision Document

## 1. The Core Problem: The Knowledge Fragmentation Paradox
In 2026, we are consuming more information than at any point in human history. Research papers, podcast transcripts, YouTube lectures, and market intelligence reports flow into our digital space at a rate that outpaces human retention.

Despite having powerful folders, tags, and search engines, we face the **Knowledge Fragmentation Paradox**: The more we know, the less we can connect. 
- **Notes are silos**: Insights from a YouTube talk on multi-agent systems rarely link to a Lloyd's market article on digital transformation.
- **Context amnesia**: We re-discover the same insight weeks apart because our tools lack a "declarative memory."
- **Retrieval vs. Reasoning**: Traditional search (and RAG) finds fragments but cannot synthesise a "big picture" across 50 disparate sources.

### The Catalyst: 30 Apps in 30 Days
The urgency for memex was born from the "30 apps in 30 days" challenge. Building at high velocity produced a massive amount of technical and strategic research. However, once the individual projects were shipped, the underlying knowledge became dormant. The builder needed a way to ensure that every project's "research debt" was converted into "knowledge equity."

## 2. The Solution: A Machine Extension of Human Memory
Inspired by Vannevar Bush’s 1945 hypothetical "memex" device, this project is a machine extension of human memory. It is designed to store all an individual's knowledge in a way that is retrieved and associated at speed.

### The Paradigm Shift: Librarian, Not Search
The fundamental shift in memex is moving from **Search** to **Curation**:
- **Search** is a reactive pull (User: "Find X").
- **memex** is an active push (Agent: "I have ingested Source A, updated Entities X and Y, and identified a contradiction with Source B").

## 3. Mission Statement
To build a high-performance, agent-maintained personal knowledge base that transforms raw information into a structured, interlinked, and queryable "Second Brain," where knowledge compounds rather than decays.

## 4. Key Use Cases
- **Lloyd's Market Intelligence**: Tracking syndicate performance, digital strategy, and regulatory changes across years of reports.
- **AI Engineering**: Maintaining a live map of agent frameworks, design patterns, and cost-optimisation strategies.
- **Mahabharata Moments**: Building a multi-thousand-node character and concept graph for deep podcast research.
- **Personal Productivity**: A unified repository for family homelab infrastructure and personal investment logic.

## 5. The Goal: Contextual Synthesis
The ultimate success metric for memex is the quality of its **Synthesis**. It succeeds when it can answer not just "What happened?" but "What are the common threads between these ten sources and how do they impact my long-term strategy?"
