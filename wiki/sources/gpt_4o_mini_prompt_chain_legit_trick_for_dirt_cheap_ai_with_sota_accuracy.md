## Summary
This video introduces GPT-4o mini, a cost-effective AI model that rivals the performance of state-of-the-art models. It details a "prompt chain" and "fusion chain" technique that allows users to achieve high accuracy and performance comparable to more expensive models like GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonic, but at a fraction of the cost. The presenter demonstrates this by applying it to an application called 'Zero Noise' for information aggregation and recommendation.

## Key claims
- GPT-4o mini offers performance nearly identical to GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonic at a significantly lower cost (30x cheaper than GPT-4o, 20x cheaper than Claude 3.5 Sonic).
- Prompt chaining and fusion chains can be used to maintain state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance even with less expensive models like GPT-4o mini.
- The 'Zero Noise' application leverages GPT-4o mini and prompt chaining to create a nearly free agentic workflow for aggregating and recommending information.
- The trend of releasing both high-end and cost-effective secondary models by major AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) is expected to continue.
