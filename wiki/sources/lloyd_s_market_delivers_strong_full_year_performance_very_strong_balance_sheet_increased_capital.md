## Summary
The Lloyd’s market achieved strong financial results in 2025, reporting profits of £10.6bn and a gross written premium of £57.9bn. Underwriting and investment returns contributed to a strengthened balance sheet, with total capital reaching £49.8bn and the central solvency ratio increasing to 496%. Lloyd’s also launched a new five-year strategy focused on enhancing its financial edge and capital advantage for improved returns.

## Key claims
- The Lloyd’s market recorded a profit before tax of £10.6bn in 2025, an increase of 10.1% year-on-year.
- Gross written premium rose by 4.2% to £57.9bn in 2025, demonstrating volume growth despite adverse foreign exchange and a more competitive pricing environment.
- A strong underwriting result of £5.2bn was achieved, with a combined ratio of 87.6% (86.9% in 2024), reflecting disciplined underwriting.
- Investment returns significantly contributed, reaching £6.0bn (5.6%) in 2025, up from £4.9bn (4.7%) in 2024.
- The balance sheet was further strengthened, with total capital, reserves, and subordinated loan notes increasing by 5.7% to £49.8bn.
- The central solvency ratio increased to 496% (435% in 2024), and the market-wide solvency ratio stood at 200%, both well above regulatory requirements.
- Lloyd’s launched a new five-year strategy with four key drivers: leading underwriting performance, a more efficient marketplace, maximising unique capital advantage, and fostering a culture of focus, innovation, and talent.
- The major claims ratio reduced to 5.8% (7.8% in 2024), indicating relatively benign catastrophe losses in the latter part of the year.

## Concepts covered
- [[gross_written_premium_gwp]] — GWP is a primary indicator of an insurer's market share and growth. For Lloyd's, it reflects the overall volume of business transacted across the market, showcasing its reach and activity.
- [[combined_ratio]] — This metric is crucial for assessing how efficiently an insurer manages claims and operating expenses relative to its earned premiums. A lower ratio signifies better underwriting performance.
- [[underwriting_result]] — This figure highlights the technical profitability of the insurance business, providing insight into the effectiveness of risk selection and pricing independently of financial market fluctuations.
- [[investment_return]] — For insurance companies, investment returns are a significant contributor to overall profitability, complementing the underwriting result and enhancing financial strength.
- [[profit_before_tax]] — This figure represents the overall financial success and earning power of the Lloyd's market for the year. It provides a comprehensive view of profitability from both underwriting and investment activities.
- [[total_capital]] — This metric is vital as it indicates the Lloyd's market's capacity to absorb significant losses, support underwriting activities, and maintain financial stability. It underpins market confidence.
- [[return_on_capital]] — This metric demonstrates Lloyd's ability to generate attractive returns from its capital base, which is crucial for its investors and for sustaining the market's long-term financial health and growth.
- [[central_solvency_ratio]] — This ratio is critical for demonstrating the robust financial security and resilience of the entire Lloyd's market to regulators, policyholders, and external stakeholders. It ensures systemic stability.
- [[market_wide_solvency_ratio]] — This ratio offers a comprehensive overview of the financial strength and capacity of the individual underwriting entities operating within Lloyd's, complementing the central solvency position.
- [[lloyds_strategy]] — This strategy is crucial for guiding Lloyd's evolution, ensuring sustained profitability, and maintaining its position as a pre-eminent global marketplace for insurance risk amidst changing market conditions.
- [[major_claims_ratio]] — This ratio helps assess the volatility of an insurer's claims experience, distinguishing between routine losses and those from large-scale events that can significantly impact the underwriting result.
- [[underlying_combined_ratio]] — This metric is important for evaluating the core, fundamental underwriting performance and resilience of the business, as it removes the distorting effects of volatile large losses.
- [[expense_ratio]] — This ratio is a critical component of the combined ratio and directly influences underwriting profitability. Managing the expense ratio efficiently is key to competitive advantage and financial health.
- [[attritional_loss_ratio]] — This ratio offers insights into the fundamental profitability of an insurer's book of business, separate from the impact of large, infrequent, and often volatile events. It's a measure of core claims management.
- [[financial_strength_ratings]] — These ratings are crucial for building and maintaining confidence among policyholders, regulators, and market participants in Lloyd's financial stability and capacity to pay claims. They are a benchmark for security.
