## Summary
Mature parenting prioritises support over control, allowing children to learn from their mistakes. While it's difficult to witness a child experience pain, a mature parent offers assistance during these challenges. Excessive control or path-creation by parents can lead to weaker adult children, whereas enabling them to make mistakes and supporting them through the process fosters resilience.

## Key claims
- Mature parenting is defined by providing support rather than exerting control over a child.
- Allowing children to make mistakes and supporting them through the consequences is crucial for development.
- Over-controlling parenting can result in weaker adult children.
- Witnessing a child's pain is difficult, but support during these times signifies mature parenting.
