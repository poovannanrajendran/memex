## Summary
This video compares the 2024 Mercedes-Benz EQA and EQB electric vehicles, highlighting differences in pricing, range, dimensions, interior space, and driving experience. The EQB is presented as a more practical and versatile option due to its larger boot, optional third row of seats, and increased interior space, while the EQA offers a smoother, quieter ride. Both models feature Mercedes' MBUX infotainment system, with the EQB offering a slightly more unique interior design element on the dashboard.

## Key claims
- The EQB is approximately £3,000 more expensive than the EQA but offers significant advantages in space and practicality.
- Both the EQA and EQB have seen substantial improvements in electric range for 2024, with the EQA reaching up to 350 miles and the EQB exceeding 300 miles.
- The EQB offers a seven-seater configuration with a foldable third row and a larger boot capacity (495L) compared to the EQA's 340L boot.
- The interior design of both vehicles is largely identical, featuring a dual-screen MBUX infotainment system, though the EQB has a slight design variation on the dashboard.
- The EQA provides a smoother and quieter driving experience, often described as softer than the A-Class, while the EQB, despite its larger size, handles corners adequately for everyday driving.
- Mercedes-Benz uses the 'EQ' designation to signify their electric intelligence, with the last letter indicating the base model (A-Class for EQA, B-Class for EQB).
