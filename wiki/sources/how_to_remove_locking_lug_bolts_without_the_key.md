## Summary
This video demonstrates a method for removing a locking lug bolt when the key is missing. The process involves using a specialized toolkit that includes a centering guide and a hardened drill bit to create a hole in the center of the lug bolt. An extractor tool with hardened ridges is then hammered into this hole, and a torque wrench is used to unscrew the damaged lug bolt without harming the wheel.

## Key claims
- Locking lug bolts can be removed without their key using a specialized toolkit.
- The toolkit allows for drilling a centered hole into the lug bolt.
- An extractor tool is used to grip and remove the lug bolt after drilling.
- A torque wrench set to 110 foot-pounds is recommended for the removal process.
- This method avoids damage to the wheel.
