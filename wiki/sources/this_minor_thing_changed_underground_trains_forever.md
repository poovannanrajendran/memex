## Summary
The introduction of dot-matrix displays on London Underground platforms significantly improved passenger satisfaction, not by reducing wait times, but by eliminating uncertainty. Knowing the exact wait time, even if it's longer, is preferable to an unknown delay. This principle, demonstrated by British Airways and Uber, highlights the importance of managing passenger perception of time and reducing anxiety through clear information.

## Key claims
- Dot-matrix displays on London Underground platforms improved passenger satisfaction by reducing uncertainty about train arrival times.
- Passengers are happier waiting a known amount of time (e.g., 9 minutes) than an unknown, potentially shorter, amount of time (e.g., 4 minutes).
- Simply stating 'delayed' on a departure board is worse than providing an estimated delay time, as it increases uncertainty and frustration.
- Companies like Uber demonstrate the value of reducing uncertainty by providing real-time information, even if it doesn't reduce the actual waiting time.
