## Summary
Jack Ma, despite admitting his lack of knowledge in technology and management, emphasises the critical importance of teamwork and vision in achieving success. His core strategy involves identifying and assembling individuals who are smarter than him in specific domains, and his primary role then becomes ensuring these talented individuals collaborate effectively. He posits that while 'stupid' people might work together easily, smart people often struggle to collaborate unless they truly believe in a shared vision, highlighting the necessity of both talent and a common purpose.

## Key claims
- It is not necessary to be an expert in all fields; instead, one should find and leverage the expertise of others who are smarter.
- The primary role of a leader is to facilitate collaboration among smart individuals.
- Smart people often struggle to work together if they do not share a common vision.
- A belief in a shared vision is crucial for enabling collaboration among intelligent individuals.
