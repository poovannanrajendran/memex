## Summary
This video provides first impressions of Apple's new iPhone 16 and 16 Pro models, alongside updates to the AirPods and Apple Watch. Key hardware changes include minor design tweaks, new camera capabilities like spatial video recording, and a new 'camera control' button. Performance is boosted by new A18 chips, and battery life is expected to improve. However, a significant point of discussion is the 'great separation' between hardware releases and the availability of Apple Intelligence features, which will not be present at launch. The Apple Watch Series 10 and Ultra 2 see minor refinements, while AirPods receive design updates and noise cancellation for the base model. The AirPods Max, however, are criticized for a lack of significant updates despite a price increase and a port change.

## Key claims
- The iPhone 16 and 16 Pro feature minor hardware updates but significant new functionalities like the camera control button.
- Apple Intelligence features will not be available on the new iPhones at launch, creating a separation between hardware and software features.
- The Apple Watch Series 10 and Ultra 2 have received incremental updates, focusing on thinner designs and improved displays.
- New base AirPods models offer design refreshes and active noise cancellation, while AirPods Pro 2 gain hearing aid certification.
- The AirPods Max are heavily criticized for lacking substantial upgrades, particularly the retention of a lightning port and a dated design, despite a price point of $550.
- The 'camera control' button is a notable new hardware feature across all iPhone 16 models, offering pressure sensitivity and gesture controls for camera functions.
- The iPhone 16 base model is suggested to be a better value proposition for most users compared to the Pro models.
