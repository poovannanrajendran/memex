## Summary
JL Collins argues that a house should not be considered an investment because its primary purpose is shelter and lifestyle, not financial gain. He highlights the substantial ongoing expenses associated with homeownership, such as mortgage payments, property taxes, maintenance, and renovations, which often outweigh any potential increase in property value. Collins advocates for owning assets like VTSAX and renting instead, suggesting this approach better facilitates wealth building for younger generations.

## Key claims
- A house is not an investment; it is a place to live and a lifestyle choice.
- Homeownership involves significant expenses beyond the mortgage, including property taxes, maintenance, and renovations.
- Owning a house, especially for wealth-building goals, is not financially optimal compared to investing in assets like VTSAX.
- The decision to own a house should be based on lifestyle preferences, not financial strategy.
