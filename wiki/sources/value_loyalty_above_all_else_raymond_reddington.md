## Summary
Raymond Reddington recounts a formative experience from his youth where he took a summer job installing carpets. Despite disliking the work, his father's advice and his own commitment to his word led him to complete the entire summer. At the end of the job, his employer, Albert Kolan, recognized his dedication and rewarded him with a bonus, teaching Reddington the invaluable lesson that loyalty should be prioritized above all else.

## Key claims
- Loyalty is a paramount value that should be upheld even in difficult circumstances.
- Commitment to one's word can lead to unexpected rewards and valuable life lessons.
- Adversity and challenging experiences can foster personal growth and strengthen character.
