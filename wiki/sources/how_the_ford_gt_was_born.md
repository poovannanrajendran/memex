## Summary
This source details the ambitious, albeit initially unsuccessful, attempt by Ford to beat Ferrari in European racing with the GT40. Facing a tight deadline and significant engineering challenges, Ford enlisted a British motorsport company to develop a car capable of over 200 mph and extreme reliability for the 24 Hours of Le Mans. Despite the challenges and initial race failures, the project eventually led to the iconic Ford GT.

## Key claims
- Ford sought European expertise by hiring a British motorsport company to develop the GT40.
- The GT40 was designed to race at over 200 mph and be reliable for the 24 Hours of Le Mans.
- The development of the GT40 was undertaken within an extremely tight 10-month deadline.
- The GT40's design featured a sleek shape with a body standing only 40 inches tall.
- Initial testing revealed the GT40 was fast but terrifyingly unstable.
- Despite Ford's efforts, all GT40s failed during the 1964 race due to breakdowns or fires, allowing Ferrari to win.
