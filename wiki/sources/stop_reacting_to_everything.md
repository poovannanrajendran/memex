## Summary
This video discusses the principle of non-interference in achieving goals and maintaining emotional stability. It suggests that clinging to desired outcomes or pushing away undesired ones creates ripples that distract from the main objective. The core advice is to avoid unnecessary reactions, both positive and negative, to external stimuli while pursuing a goal, to conserve energy and maintain focus.

## Key claims
- Trying to hold onto things leads to them moving away.
- Trying to avoid things leads to them moving closer.
- Touching the 'water' (engaging with external stimuli) initiates ripples, which can distract from a goal.
- Avoid reacting to things simply because you like or dislike them when pursuing a goal.
- Unnecessary reactions drain energy that should be directed towards the initial goal.
