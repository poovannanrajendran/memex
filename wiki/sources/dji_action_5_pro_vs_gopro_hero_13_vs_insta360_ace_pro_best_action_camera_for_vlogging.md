## Summary
This video compares the DJI Osmo Action 5 Pro, DJI Osmo Action 4, GoPro Hero 13 Black, and Insta360 Ace Pro action cameras. It evaluates image and audio quality, highlighting unique features like HDR capabilities, 10-bit and log color support, stabilization, slow-motion options, fields of view, zoom, macro capabilities, low-light performance, and microphone inputs. The DJI Action 5 Pro emerges as a strong contender due to its built-in storage, dual microphone support, and excellent battery life, although its HDR effect in normal mode is noted as potentially heavy-handed. The GoPro Hero 13 Black stands out for its interchangeable lenses and built-in GPS, while the Insta360 Ace Pro features a unique flip screen and 8K recording, though it lacks advanced color features.

## Key claims
- The DJI Osmo Action 5 Pro features a new sensor and processor, offering more vibrant colours and balanced lighting compared to the Action 4 and GoPro Hero 13 in normal 4K colour mode.
- The Action 5 Pro and Hero 13 both offer HLG (Hybrid Log Gamma) HDR, which is superior to the Ace Pro's standard HDR, while the Action 4 lacks an HDR mode.
- The Insta360 Ace Pro lacks 10-bit colour and log colour, making it less suitable for professional colour grading in post-production compared to the other cameras.
- The GoPro Hero 13 Black is the only action camera with built-in GPS and offers interchangeable lenses (ultra-wide, macro, anamorphic) and ND filters with auto-detection.
- The DJI Osmo Action 5 Pro's standout features include built-in storage, the ability to connect two DJI Mic 2 transmitters simultaneously, and a built-in pressure gauge for depth and altitude.
- The DJI Osmo Action 5 Pro boasts the best battery life among the tested cameras, with continuous recording of at least 2.5 hours in 4K 30fps using its new battery.
- The Insta360 Ace Pro has a flip screen, offers up to 8K resolution recording, and features unique usability features like pausing/cancelling recording and gesture controls.
