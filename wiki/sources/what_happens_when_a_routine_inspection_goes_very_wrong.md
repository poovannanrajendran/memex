## Summary
A seemingly routine IT network setup trip for a new office location uncovered severe structural and safety issues in the building. Despite a 5-year lease, the building lacked functioning air conditioning, basic utilities in some areas, and critical safety systems like fire alarms and sprinklers. Investigations revealed a complete absence of necessary building permits for major renovations, leading to the building's immediate shutdown by city and fire authorities and resulting in legal action against the landlord.

## Key claims
- The landlord of a new office location refused to install network wiring, leading the company's IT worker to install it themselves.
- The building was unfinished upon occupancy, lacking basic functionalities like air conditioning and running water in some areas.
- The building had no functioning fire alarm system and the main sprinkler system lacked water pressure.
- Major renovations (new walls, plumbing, electrical, fire system adjustments) were done without any required building permits.
- City and fire inspectors declared the entire building unsafe, leading to its immediate shutdown and loss of occupancy approval.
- The landlord attempted to enforce a 5-year lease despite the building being unsafe and unfit for use.
- The company successfully broke the lease, retrieved their deposit and rent, and is now seeking compensation for damages.

## Concepts covered
- [[building_permits]] — Essential for ensuring building safety, structural integrity, and legal occupancy, protecting occupants and preventing liability. In this case, their complete absence for major renovations directly led to the building's shutdown.
- [[occupancy_approval]] — Crucial for the legal and safe operation of a building. Its loss in this case meant the building could not be legally or safely used, rendering the lease voidable.
- [[lease_agreement]] — Defines the legal relationship and obligations between a tenant and landlord. In this scenario, the landlord attempted to use it to enforce occupancy of an unsafe building, which was challenged through legal means.
- [[building_safety_violations]] — Directly impacts the safety and well-being of building occupants. Serious violations, as found in this source, can lead to forced evacuations, fines, legal action, and complete building shutdowns.
- [[hvac_system_air_conditioning]] — Essential for occupant comfort, health, and productivity, especially in office environments. Its failure in the new building highlighted its unfinished state and significantly impacted the IT team's ability to work.
- [[fire_safety_system]] — Absolutely critical for protecting lives and property in the event of a fire. The complete failure of this system, including non-functional alarms and unpressurised sprinklers, was a primary reason for the building's immediate shutdown.

## Contradictions or open questions
