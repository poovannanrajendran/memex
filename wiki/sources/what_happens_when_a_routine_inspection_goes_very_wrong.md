## Summary
A routine IT network setup project at a new building quickly escalated when numerous severe safety and structural issues were uncovered. These included a non-functional air conditioning system, ongoing construction, and critically, damaged or non-existent fire safety systems and a complete lack of building permits for major renovations. City and fire department inspections ultimately declared the entire building unsafe, leading to its shutdown, the termination of a 5-year lease, and potential legal action against the landlord for compensation.

## Key claims
- A newly leased building, intended for IT network setup, was found to be unfinished and structurally unsafe.
- The landlord refused to install network wiring, necessitating the IT worker to perform the extensive cabling personally.
- The building suffered from critical infrastructure failures, including non-functional air conditioning, lack of running water in some areas, and ongoing construction.
- Significant safety hazards were identified, such as damaged fire sprinklers, disconnected smoke detectors, and a complete absence of building permits for major renovations.
- Official inspections by city and fire department authorities confirmed severe violations, leading to the immediate declaration of the building as unsafe and its subsequent shutdown.
- The tenant successfully terminated a 5-year lease due to the building's hazardous condition and is pursuing compensation from the landlord for incurred costs and effort.

## Concepts covered
- [[building_permits]] — Crucial for ensuring the safety, structural integrity, and legal compliance of any building work. Their absence indicates unapproved and potentially unsafe modifications, as seen in this case.
- [[occupancy_approval]] — Essential for the legal and safe use of any property. Without it, a building cannot be lawfully occupied, directly impacting tenant rights and safety.
- [[fire_sprinkler_system]] — A critical life safety feature in commercial buildings, vital for protecting occupants and property from the devastating effects of fire. A non-functional system poses an extreme and unacceptable risk.
- [[fire_alarm_system]] — Crucial for providing early warning of fire, enabling timely evacuation and emergency response, which is paramount for saving lives and minimizing property damage.
- [[lease_agreement]] — Establishes the rights, responsibilities, and obligations for both landlords and tenants. In this context, its enforceability was challenged due to the landlord's failure to provide a safe and habitable property.
