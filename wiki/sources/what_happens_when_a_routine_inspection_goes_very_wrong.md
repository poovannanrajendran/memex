## Summary
An IT worker's routine trip to install network lines in a new building rapidly escalated into the discovery of serious safety violations. Despite the landlord's initial refusal to address concerns and attempt to enforce a five-year lease, investigations by a city inspector and the fire department revealed critical issues, including a non-functional fire alarm and sprinkler system. This ultimately led to the entire building being declared unsafe and shut down, with the company now seeking compensation for the wasted time and costs.

## Key claims
- A building intended for lease was presented as finished but lacked basic functionalities like working air conditioning and running water in some areas, indicating it was far from ready for occupancy.
- Major structural and system changes, including new walls, plumbing, electrical work, and fire system adjustments, were undertaken without obtaining the necessary building permits.
- The building's fire safety systems were critically compromised, with damaged fire sprinklers, a missing/disconnected fire alarm panel, unconnected smoke detectors, and crucially, no water pressure in the main sprinkler system.
- These severe building safety violations, once reported to authorities, led to the building losing its approval for occupancy and being immediately declared unsafe and shut down by the fire department.
- The landlord attempted to enforce a long-term lease on a demonstrably unsafe and unready building, but after the official inspections, was forced to return the deposit and rent, with the aggrieved company now pursuing further compensation.
