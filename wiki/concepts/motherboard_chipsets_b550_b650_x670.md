## Definition
Chipsets are integrated circuits on the motherboard that manage data flow between the CPU, memory, storage, and peripherals. B550 and B650 are typically mid-range chipsets, while X670 is a higher-end chipset offering more connectivity and features.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The chipset determines the motherboard's compatibility with specific CPUs, features like PCIe generation support, number of USB ports, and overall expandability, directly impacting performance and functionality.

## Key properties or components
- PCIe lanes and generation support (e.g., PCIe 4.0, 5.0)
- Number and type of USB ports
- SATA port count
- M.2 slot support
- Overclocking capabilities
- Connectivity options (Wi-Fi, Ethernet)
