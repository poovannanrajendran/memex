## Definition
The market-wide solvency ratio reflects the aggregated capital position of all syndicates within the Lloyd's market relative to their total capital requirements. It is a consolidated view of solvency.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
This ratio offers a comprehensive overview of the financial strength and capacity of the individual underwriting entities operating within Lloyd's, complementing the central solvency position.

## Key properties or components
- Decreased slightly to 200% in 2025 (from 205% in 2024).
- Remains well above regulatory requirements.
