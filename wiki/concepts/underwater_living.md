## Definition
The act of residing in an environment fully submerged in water for an extended duration. This typically requires specialised equipment and significant physiological and psychological adaptation.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Demonstrates extreme human endurance and the ability to adapt to challenging environments, pushing the boundaries of what is considered possible for the human body.

## Key properties or components
- Requires constant supply of breathable air
- Necessitates adaptation to water pressure
- Involves specialised living quarters or enclosures
- Tests psychological resilience

## Contradictions or debates
While Cem Karabay lived underwater for 8 days, this is a short-term feat and not a sustainable or natural mode of human habitation.
