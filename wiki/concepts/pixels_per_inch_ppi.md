## Definition
Pixels Per Inch (PPI) is a measure of display resolution, indicating the number of pixels a display has within a linear inch. Higher PPI results in sharper images and text.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Affects the clarity of text and numbers on screen. A recommended PPI of around 220 or higher makes small details easier to discern for data scientists.

## Key properties or components
- Display resolution
- Pixel density
- Image sharpness
- Text clarity
