## Definition
Recording video at a high frame rate (e.g., 120fps, 240fps, 960fps) and playing it back at a standard frame rate (e.g., 30fps) to create a slow-motion effect.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Adds dramatic or artistic effect to footage, allowing viewers to observe fast action in detail.

## Key properties or components
- High frame rates (fps)
- Super slow motion
- Resolution trade-offs

## Contradictions or debates
None.
