## Definition
Shutter speed is the length of time the camera's shutter is open to expose the sensor to light. A faster shutter speed (e.g., 1/18000th of a second) freezes motion, while a slower shutter speed allows more light and can create motion blur.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The extremely fast shutter speed used was critical in enabling the photographer to capture the fast-moving bullet.

## Key properties or components
- Controls exposure
- Manages motion blur
- Measured in seconds or fractions of a second
- Impacts image brightness
