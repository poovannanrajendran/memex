## Definition
A comparison between financial resources and the passage of time, asserting that wealth cannot overcome the limitations imposed by time's unidirectional flow. It challenges the notion that money can solve all problems, particularly those related to existence and mortality.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
This contrast is the core message of the quote, directly addressing the common human desire for more time and questioning whether financial means can procure it.

## Key properties or components
- Purchasability
- Limitations of money
- Irreversibility of time
- Human desires

## Contradictions or debates
None.
