## Definition
The amount of text included in an AI prompt, often measured in tokens or words. The assumption that longer prompts always yield better results is examined.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The source debunks the myth that longer prompts are always better, highlighting research that shows a decline in reasoning performance with excessive prompt length due to information overload.

## Key properties or components
- Token count
- Context inclusion
- Information density

## Contradictions or debates
Contrary to popular belief, excessively long prompts can degrade AI performance by making it difficult to prioritize relevant information.
