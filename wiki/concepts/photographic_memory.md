## Definition
Photographic memory, also known as eidetic memory, is the ability to recall images, sounds, or objects in memory with extreme precision and in abundant volume. It allows an individual to recall a memory in great detail, as if they are reliving the experience.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
This concept is crucial as it defines a key characteristic and plot device for the character Mike Ross in the series Suits, enabling dramatic and plot-driving moments.

## Key properties or components
- Extreme detail recall
- High volume memory
- Vivid sensory recall
- Often debated in scientific circles

## Contradictions or debates
The existence and exact nature of true photographic memory in humans is a subject of scientific debate, with many researchers questioning if it is a distinct ability or an extreme form of standard memory.
