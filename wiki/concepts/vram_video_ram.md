## Definition
Dedicated memory on a graphics card used to store textures, frame buffers, and other graphical data. More VRAM is often required for higher resolutions, complex scenes, and demanding applications like video editing and 3D rendering.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Identified as a critical factor for video editing performance, where 12GB of VRAM on the Titan Xp proved sufficient, preventing crashes experienced with lower VRAM GPUs.

## Key properties or components
- Capacity (GB)
- Bandwidth (GB/s)
- Type (GDDR5, GDDR6, HBM2)
- Impacts texture loading and rendering speed
