## Definition
A set of principles that describe how the human eye perceives visual elements when attempting to make sense of the world. They suggest that the whole is often perceived as more than the sum of its parts.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Crucial for understanding user perception in design, enabling the creation of visually coherent, intuitive, and aesthetically pleasing interfaces and brands.

## Key properties or components
- Proximity
- Similarity
- Closure
- Continuity
- Figure/Ground
- Common Fate
- Symmetry

## Contradictions or debates
While generally accepted, the application and interpretation of Gestalt principles can be subjective and may vary across different cultures or individual experiences.
