## Definition
The brain's ability to reorganize itself by forming new neural connections throughout life. It allows the brain to adapt to new information, experiences, and changes in its environment.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Underpins the idea that the brain can change and adapt based on new inputs and thought patterns, suggesting that consciously changing one's mindset could lead to tangible changes in brain function and, consequently, physical health.

## Key properties or components
- Adaptability
- Learning
- Memory formation
- Functional recovery
