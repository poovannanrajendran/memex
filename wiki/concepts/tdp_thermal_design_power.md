## Definition
TDP, or Thermal Design Power, is a measure of the maximum amount of heat a component, typically a CPU or GPU, is expected to generate under typical workloads. It guides the selection of appropriate cooling solutions.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
A key specification for determining the required cooling capacity of a CPU, directly influencing the choice of CPU cooler.

## Key properties or components
- Heat generation metric
- Indicator for cooling needs
- Maximum power consumption under load
