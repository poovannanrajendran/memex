## Definition
Ultra-wideband (UWB) is a short-range wireless technology that uses a wide frequency spectrum for high-bandwidth, low-power communications. It is particularly adept at precise location tracking and secure ranging.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The source mentions that certain Cisco access points include UWB, enabling advanced location-based services such as indoor navigation and smart space management.

## Key properties or components
- Precise location tracking
- Short-range communication
- High bandwidth
- Low power consumption
