## Definition
Wi-Fi 7, also known as IEEE 802.11be, is the next generation of Wi-Fi technology that promises significantly higher speeds, lower latency, and increased capacity compared to previous Wi-Fi standards.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The source announces Cisco's new access points that support this latest Wi-Fi standard, indicating a move towards faster and more robust wireless connectivity.

## Key properties or components
- Higher throughput
- Lower latency
- Increased capacity
- Advanced features like Multi-Link Operation (MLO)
