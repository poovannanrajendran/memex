## Definition
VRMs are crucial components on a motherboard responsible for converting and supplying stable voltage from the power supply to the CPU. They consist of MOSFETs, chokes, and capacitors.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Adequate VRMs are essential for stable CPU operation, especially during overclocking or when running power-hungry CPUs. Poor VRMs can lead to throttling, instability, and even damage to the CPU.

## Key properties or components
- Phase count
- Component quality (MOSFETs, capacitors)
- Cooling solutions (heatsinks)
- Power delivery efficiency

## Contradictions or debates
Higher phase counts do not always equate to better performance if the quality of individual components or the cooling is insufficient.
