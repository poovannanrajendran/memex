## Definition
A perpetual soup is a type of soup that is continuously maintained by regularly adding fresh ingredients and water to a base that is kept simmering. The original broth is never discarded entirely, allowing flavours to deepen over a long period.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
This concept is central to the restaurant's unique offering and its longevity, demonstrating a novel approach to food preparation and flavour development.

## Key properties or components
- Continuous simmering
- Daily addition of fresh ingredients
- Never-discarded base broth
- Deepening flavour complexity
- Reduced bacterial growth
