## Definition
Negative emotions are psychological states that are generally considered unpleasant, such as anger, sadness, fear, guilt, and shame. While they can be uncomfortable, they often serve as signals or motivators for change.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Understanding and managing negative emotions is vital for mental well-being, decision-making, and overall life success, as unchecked negative emotions can lead to destructive behaviours and thought patterns.

## Key properties or components
- Subjective unpleasantness
- Signal function
- Potential for destructiveness
- Impact on behaviour and cognition

## Contradictions or debates
Some argue that all emotions are neutral and serve a purpose, and the 'negativity' is a societal construct. Others suggest that experiencing negative emotions is essential for growth and resilience.
