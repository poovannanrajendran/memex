## Definition
The central solvency ratio measures the capital held by the central fund of Lloyd's relative to the capital required to meet its financial obligations. It provides a buffer for unforeseen events.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
This ratio is critical for demonstrating the robust financial security and resilience of the entire Lloyd's market to regulators, policyholders, and external stakeholders. It ensures systemic stability.

## Key properties or components
- Increased to 496% in 2025 (from 435% in 2024).
- Remains well above regulatory requirements.
