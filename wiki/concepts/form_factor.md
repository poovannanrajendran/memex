## Definition
Motherboard form factor refers to the physical dimensions and layout of the motherboard. Common form factors include ATX, MicroATX (mATX), and Mini-ITX (ITX).

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The form factor dictates the size of the PC case the motherboard will fit into and influences the number of expansion slots (PCIe) and other features the motherboard can accommodate.

## Key properties or components
- Physical dimensions (width and height)
- Number of expansion slots
- Number of RAM slots
- Compatibility with PC cases
