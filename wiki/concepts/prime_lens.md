## Definition
A prime lens has a fixed focal length and cannot zoom. This often results in superior image quality, wider apertures, and a more compact design compared to zoom lenses.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Prime lenses are favoured by many photographers for their optical performance and specific field of view, and are central to discussions about lens choice and value.

## Key properties or components
- Fixed focal length
- Often sharper
- Wider maximum aperture
- Compact size
