## Definition
Manual settings in drone flight control refer to the direct configuration of flight parameters by the user, rather than relying on default or automatic presets. This can include aspects like flight speed, altitude, camera angles, and the specific behaviour at waypoints.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Utilising manual settings allows for fine-tuning drone behaviour to suit specific mission requirements, ensuring optimal data quality and operational safety, which is crucial for AI-driven applications.

## Key properties or components
- User-defined parameters
- Control over flight speed
- Control over altitude
- Control over camera gimbal
- Customisation of waypoint actions

## Contradictions or debates
Some users might find extensive manual configuration overly complex or time-consuming compared to automated solutions.
