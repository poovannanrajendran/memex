## Definition
A bicycle drivetrain system that uses a single chainring at the front and a wide-range cassette at the rear. It simplifies the gear selection process and reduces mechanical complexity.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The author's analysis focuses on the suitability and advantages of 1x drivetrains for gravel biking, challenging their previous assumptions.

## Key properties or components
- Single front chainring
- Wide-range rear cassette
- Simplicity
- Reduced weight
- Potential for greater tire clearance
- Fewer chain drops (generally)
