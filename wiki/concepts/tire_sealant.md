## Definition
Tire sealants are substances, typically liquid or paste-like, injected into a punctured tire to temporarily or permanently seal the leak. They often contain particles or fibers that expand and fill the hole when exposed to air.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Crucial for emergency tire repairs, allowing vehicles to continue functioning after a puncture, thereby avoiding immediate roadside changes or towing.

## Key properties or components
- Sealing particles/fibers
- Liquid or paste consistency
- Application via valve stem
- Varying effectiveness and lifespan
