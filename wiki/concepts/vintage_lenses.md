## Definition
Camera lenses manufactured in past eras, often from the mid-20th century. These lenses are sought after for their unique optical characteristics, such as distinct bokeh and character, which differ from modern lenses.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
The Helios 44-2 is a prime example of a vintage lens being repurposed for modern, high-budget film production, challenging the notion that only new, expensive equipment is suitable for professional results.

## Key properties or components
- Unique optical flaws (e.g., swirly bokeh)
- Often manual focus
- Potentially lower sharpness compared to modern lenses
- Affordable acquisition cost
