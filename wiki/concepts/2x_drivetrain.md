## Definition
A bicycle drivetrain system that uses two chainrings at the front and a cassette at the rear. This offers a wider overall gear range and potentially tighter gear spacing between adjacent cogs.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Historically considered the standard for versatile road and gravel bikes, the author contrasts the perceived benefits of 2x systems with the emerging advantages of 1x.

## Key properties or components
- Two front chainrings
- Standard rear cassette
- Wider overall gear range
- Tighter gear spacing
- More complex operation
- Potential for more chain drops
