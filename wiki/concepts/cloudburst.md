## Definition
A cloudburst is an extreme amount of rain that falls in a short period, often in mountainous areas. It is characterised by a sudden and heavy downpour that can lead to flash floods and landslides.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
Understanding cloudbursts is crucial for disaster preparedness, especially in regions prone to extreme weather events, as they can cause significant damage to infrastructure like railway tracks.

## Key properties or components
- Sudden and intense rainfall
- Short duration
- Associated with flash floods and landslides
- Often occurs in specific geographical conditions
