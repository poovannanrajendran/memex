## Definition
The phenomenon where an individual, through prolonged immersion in a role (especially a professional one like a surgeon), begins to internalise aspects of that role, potentially leading to a distorted perception of reality or their own capabilities.

## Why it matters (in Poovi's context)
This concept explains Highmore's humorous concern that playing a surgeon might make him think he could actually perform surgery, underscoring the psychological impact of deep role immersion.

## Key properties or components
- Immersion
- Internalisation
- Distorted perception
- Transfer of perceived skills
