## Overview
AMD's Threadripper processors are high-end desktop (HEDT) CPUs designed for extreme performance, featuring very high core counts (up to 64 cores/128 threads) and extensive platform capabilities. They are targeted at professionals and enthusiasts needing significant computational power.

## Role in this knowledge base
Mentioned as a high-performance upgrade option, but considered too expensive due to platform costs (motherboard, RAM) for a budget build.

## Key facts
- The creator noted that a Threadripper upgrade would require a new motherboard and power supply, making it a costly option.
