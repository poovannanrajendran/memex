## Overview
H.265, also known as HEVC (High Efficiency Video Coding), is a video compression standard designed to provide significantly better compression than its predecessor, H.264. It offers higher quality at lower bitrates but requires more processing power for encoding and decoding.

## Role in this knowledge base
Mentioned as the codec of the creator's original footage, which contributes to the high demands placed on their editing system.

## Key facts
- The creator acknowledges that editing H.265 footage directly without transcoding is challenging and contributes to performance issues on less powerful systems.
